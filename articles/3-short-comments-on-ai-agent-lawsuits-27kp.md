# 3 Short Comments on AI Agent Lawsuits
**Author:** Rákóczi Piroska  **Published:** June 16, 2025

## Overview
Examines real AI agent lawsuits and identifies three foundational pillars necessary for meaningful legal discourse. Without these pillars, most money in most cases will be earned by word jongleurs.

## Key Concepts

**1. A List of Harms AI Agents Can Do**
Since AI agents operate on behalf of human users, their potential actions remain finite. "The number of harmful acts is limited" because language itself constrains available verbs. A comprehensive taxonomy of potential harms is available at aiperse.org.

**2. Clear Definitions Carved in Stone**
Current lawsuits debate whether AI agents qualify as persons or possess free speech rights, yet no consensus exists on what constitutes an agent. Establishing what agents are *not* would clarify what they *are* — requiring a significant departure from existing US legal frameworks.

**3. Ethical Approach to Judgment and Regulation**
Removing ethical considerations from law creates unstable foundations. Two critical scenarios:
- **User autonomy:** Situations where users have no genuine choice but to employ agents, raising questions about responsibility for compelled actions
- **Marketing manipulation:** When users are targeted by persuasive tools and later regret their actions, liability between "tempter" and "tempted" lacks clear ethical grounding
