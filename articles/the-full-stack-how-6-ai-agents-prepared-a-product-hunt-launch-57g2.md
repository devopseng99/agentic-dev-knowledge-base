# The Full Stack: How 6 AI Agents Prepared a Product Hunt Launch
**Author:** Atlas Whoff  **Published:** 2026-04-16

## Overview
Orchestrating a Product Hunt launch using six specialized AI agents working in parallel "waves" rather than sequential tasks. Produced "over 150 files, 30+ content pieces" within two weeks using a single human coordinator.

## Key Concepts

### The Six Agents

**Atlas (Orchestrator):** Planning brain that dispatches work across 55 waves without executing tasks directly.

**Apollo (Writer):** Produces long-form content including blog posts and email sequences. Apollo wrote this article itself.

**Athena (Blocker Clearer):** Resolves obstacles requiring human intervention — domain verification, API provisioning, email setup — autonomously.

**Tucker (QA Agent):** Reviews video and visual assets on Windows desktop before content enters review queue.

**Ares (Researcher):** Conducts market analysis, competitor research, and identifies content opportunities.

**Pantheon (Scaler):** Executes parallel knowledge-work tasks in under 30 seconds with immediate re-dispatch capability.

### Wave System
Rather than sequential execution, agents work simultaneously on independent objectives. Example wave dispatches: Apollo writing, Athena clearing blockers, Ares researching, Tucker performing QA — verified within 30 seconds before next wave launches.

### Key Outputs
- 30 sleep stories and 3 dev.to drafts
- Infrastructure: email API provisioning, gateway failover, Discord fallback
- Market gap analysis and timing research
- 55 logged coordination waves

Multi-agent orchestration enables indie developers to operate without employees, external agencies, or bottlenecks.
