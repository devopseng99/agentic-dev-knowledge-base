# Agentic vs Graph RAG: Two Paths to Smarter AI Systems

**Author:** Muhammad Mairaj
**Published:** September 25, 2025

## Overview

Compares Agentic RAG and Graph RAG as two approaches to improving retrieval-augmented generation beyond basic vector database search. Advocates combining both for optimal systems.

## Key Concepts

### Agentic RAG

Gives language models autonomous decision-making capabilities. The model actively determines what information to seek, how to find it, and when to conclude. "It's like the difference between a librarian fetching you a single book and a research assistant who knows your goal."

Offers flexibility and goal-driven iteration but introduces complexity through potential loops, dead ends, and hallucinations.

### Graph RAG

Structures knowledge as interconnected entities and relationships rather than raw text. "You have a map of how ideas fit together" instead of disconnected chunks. Creates more grounded, resilient answers since relationships are explicit.

### Hybrid Approach

Combining both creates optimal systems -- agents for flexibility paired with knowledge graphs for stability. "Retrieval is the foundation of every serious AI system."
