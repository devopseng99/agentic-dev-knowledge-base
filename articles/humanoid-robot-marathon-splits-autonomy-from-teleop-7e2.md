# Humanoid Robot Marathon Splits Autonomy From Teleop
**Author:** Simon Paxton  **Published:** April 13, 2026

## Overview
Beijing's 21-kilometer humanoid robot marathon represents a shift toward measurable robotics benchmarking. More than 70 teams competed in an overnight full-course test, with the event notable for separating autonomous and remote-controlled categories rather than mixing them.

## Key Concepts
- **Autonomous vs. Teleoperated distinction:** Clear separation shows which intelligence resides on the machine versus with human operators
- **Endurance testing:** 21km course reveals battery limitations, actuator wear, and failure modes invisible in short demonstrations
- **Auditable control:** Remote-operated runs face a 1.2x time penalty multiplier, making operator dependence visible
- **Repeatable benchmarking:** Regular scheduled events create standardized metrics that are difficult to game
- **Data infrastructure:** Telemetry across dozens of teams generates analysis opportunities for robotics optimization

Approximately 40% of participating teams attempted fully autonomous navigation.
