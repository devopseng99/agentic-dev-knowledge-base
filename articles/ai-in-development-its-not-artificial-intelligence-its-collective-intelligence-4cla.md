# AI in Development: It's Not Artificial Intelligence, It's Collective Intelligence
**Author:** Vladimir Semenov  **Published:** December 18, 2025

## Overview
Senior software engineer with nearly 30 years of experience reframes AI tools as mechanisms for accessing "collective intelligence" rather than autonomous artificial intelligence.

## Key Concepts

### Core Argument
"When a Software Engineer uses AI for work, it's not really 'AI' helping them; rather, it is many engineers all around the world working together to help them."

AI tools synthesize patterns from millions of public repositories and decades of developer experience — essentially large-scale asynchronous pair programming.

### Key Points
- AI tools = distilled knowledge from global developer communities
- Using AI resembles large-scale asynchronous pair programming
- Engineers serve as gatekeepers, validating collective input for quality and security
- AI adoption = augmenting individual engineers with "the digitised wisdom of every engineer who came before them"

### Conclusion
This is profoundly human collaboration enabled by technology, not human replacement.
