# The 2025 Compliance Checklist for AI-Driven Companies
**Author:** Ray Parker  **Published:** July 5, 2025

## Overview
AI-driven organizations must navigate evolving regulatory, ethical, and privacy requirements. Compliance has become strategically critical for building stakeholder trust and avoiding penalties.

## Key Concepts

**The 10-Point AI Compliance Checklist**

1. **Regulatory Alignment by Jurisdiction**
   - EU AI Act: Risk-level classification and mandatory documentation
   - U.S. Algorithmic Accountability Act: Impact assessments for critical-decision systems
   - China's AI Regulations: Algorithm registration and content moderation mandates
   - Global: GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, India's DPDP Bill

2. **Bias Mitigation and Fairness Auditing**
   - Tools: IBM AI Fairness 360, Microsoft Fairlearn, Google What-If Tool

3. **Explainability and Transparency**
   - Incorporate XAI layers like LIME, SHAP, or model cards

4. **Secure Data Collection and Consent Management**
   - Tools: OneTrust, Osano, custom consent-tracking APIs

5. **Continuous Model Monitoring and Lifecycle Audits**
   - Platforms: Arize AI, MLflow, Weights & Biases

6. **Incident Response and Redress Mechanisms**
   - Create user-facing appeal options for sensitive decisions
   - Executive-level accountability (Chief AI Ethics Officer role)

7. **Third-Party and Vendor Accountability**
   - Request vendor compliance proof (SOC 2, ISO 27001)

8. **Employee Training and Governance Culture**
   - Cross-functional AI Ethics Committees

9. **Algorithmic Accountability and Documentation**
   - Full decision traceability; version-controlled change documentation

10. **Sustainability and Environmental Impact**
    - Energy measurement for training/inference; green cloud provider selection

**Key Insight:** Small startups still need to follow compliance rules when user data is involved. Open-source tools are NOT compliant by default.
