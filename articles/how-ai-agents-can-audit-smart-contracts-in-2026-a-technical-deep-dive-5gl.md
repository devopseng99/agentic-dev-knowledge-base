## Overview
A technical exploration of how AI agents are used to audit smart contracts — identifying vulnerabilities, logic errors, and security risks in blockchain-based agreements. In 2026, AI agents aren't just assisting developers — they're autonomously writing, auditing, and testing smart contracts.

## Key Concepts

**Why Smart Contract Auditing Matters**
Smart contracts are immutable once deployed. Bugs can lead to catastrophic financial losses (e.g., The DAO hack, various DeFi exploits). Manual auditing is expensive, slow, and human-error prone.

**AI Agent Approaches to Smart Contract Auditing**
1. Static Analysis: AI scans contract code for known vulnerability patterns (reentrancy, integer overflow, access control issues)
2. Formal Verification: AI generates mathematical proofs of contract correctness
3. Fuzzing: AI generates edge case inputs to discover unexpected behaviors
4. Semantic Analysis: AI understands contract intent and compares against implementation

**Common Vulnerabilities AI Can Detect**
- Reentrancy attacks
- Integer overflow/underflow
- Unprotected SELFDESTRUCT calls
- Front-running vulnerabilities
- Oracle manipulation risks

**Key Benefits of AI-Driven Auditing**
- Speed: AI can analyze thousands of lines in seconds vs. days for humans
- Consistency: No human fatigue or oversight gaps
- Coverage: Can test more edge cases than manual review
- Cost: Dramatically reduces audit costs
