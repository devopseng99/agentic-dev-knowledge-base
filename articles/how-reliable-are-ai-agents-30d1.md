# How reliable are AI agents?
**Author:** Priya Negi  **Published:** November 6, 2025

## Overview
Examines AI agent reliability in the rapidly evolving agentic AI landscape, defining reliability as "the consistent ability of autonomous systems to complete intended tasks without causing unintended consequences, even in unpredictable environments."

## Key Concepts
1. **Definition of Reliability** — Distinguishing between hard errors and subtle quality regressions in agent performance
2. **Five Reliability Dimensions:**
   - Consistency in producing logical outcomes
   - Accuracy and avoidance of hallucinations
   - Robustness and resilience to unexpected inputs
   - Policy alignment and compliance boundaries
   - Auditability and explainability of decisions
3. **Reliability Challenges:**
   - Autonomous decision-making without fixed instructions
   - Multi-system integration complexity
   - Security vulnerabilities (prompt injection, memory poisoning)
   - Operational scaling costs
4. **Browser vs. Mobile Agents Comparison** — Mobile agents demonstrate superior reliability due to controlled OS environments and cleaner interfaces, despite challenges with cross-app coordination
