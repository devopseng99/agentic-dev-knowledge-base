# What is physical AI - is it more than just hype?
**Author:** YK Sugi  **Published:** May 7, 2026

## Overview
Physical AI represents AI systems that perceive environments through sensors, reason about them, and execute physical actions with genuine adaptability rather than pre-programmed responses. The article examines whether this emerging field constitutes legitimate technological advancement or marketing rebranding.

## Key Concepts
1. **Vision-Language-Action (VLA) Models** - End-to-end systems fusing perception, planning, and control into single models that convert camera input and language instructions into motor commands (paradigm established by Google DeepMind's RT-2 in 2023)
2. **World Models** - Neural networks trained on millions of hours of real-world video data to understand physics and cause-and-effect relationships
3. **Sim-to-Real Transfer** - Training robots in physics-accurate digital environments before deploying to physical settings
4. **Multimodal Perception** - Processing information from cameras, lidar, force sensors, and language simultaneously
5. **Edge and Cloud Inference** - Models running locally for latency-sensitive tasks and in cloud for higher-level reasoning

## Market Data
- Market reached ~$5 billion in 2025, projected to reach $68-84 billion by 2034-35
- Robot unit costs dropped 30x over past decade (from ~$3 million to ~$100,000)
- Projected humanoid robot costs: $35,000 (2025) declining to $13,000-$17,000 within next decade

- Waymo: Over 10 million paid robotaxi rides completed
- Amazon: Deployed millionth robot with 10% fleet efficiency improvement
