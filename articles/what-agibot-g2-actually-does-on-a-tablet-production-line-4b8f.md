# What Agibot G2 actually does on a tablet production line
**Author:** Pomeloid  **Published:** April 16, 2026

## Overview
The article examines the Agibot G2 humanoid robot's deployment at Longcheer Technology's tablet manufacturing facility in Nanchang, China. It details the robot's specific work on Multimedia Integrated Testing (MMIT) stations and evaluates the significance of this real-world industrial application.

## Key Concepts
- **The Task:** The G2 picks tablets from stacks, navigates factory floors, inserts units into test fixtures with millimeter-level precision, waits for test results, and sorts units by outcome -- completing roughly 310 units per hour
- **Hardware:** Wheeled humanoid with 26 degrees of freedom (expandable to ~50), dual 7-DoF force-controlled arms, multimodal sensor stack including LiDAR and RGB-D cameras, IP42/IP50 ratings, hot-swappable battery packs (1,652 Wh)
- **Performance Metrics:** 310 UPH throughput, 99%+ success rate (self-reported), 140+ cumulative operation hours with <4% downtime, 36-hour integration time
- **Significance:** Demonstrates flexibility -- the robot works on existing production lines without custom end effectors or redesigned fixtures
- **Limitations:** Task-specific training, unclear failure definitions, limited long-term wear data
