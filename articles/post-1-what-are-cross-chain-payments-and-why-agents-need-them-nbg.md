# What Are Cross-Chain Payments and Why Agents Need Them

**Author:** Alex Rivers
**Published:** May 2, 2026

## Overview

Cross-chain payments aim to do for value what the internet did for information -- make it freely movable. For autonomous agents, this is fundamental infrastructure.

## Single-Chain Limitations

Traditional payment systems assume human operators making deliberate, high-value transactions with acceptable latency and tolerable fees ($2-$30). Agents execute multiple micro-transactions ($0.001 each) across different services within a single workflow without human confirmation delays.

## Why Chains Stay Separate

Each blockchain maintains sovereignty with independent consensus, validators, and ledgers. Cross-chain transfer has historically required:
- Centralized bridges (custodial risk)
- Manual intervention (human time)
- Complex multi-sig schemes (latency)

None suit machine-speed operations.

## Requirements for Agent Cross-Chain Payments

1. **Abstraction** -- Agents shouldn't need blockchain routing knowledge
2. **Speed** -- Confirmation times must align with agent timeout windows
3. **Atomicity** -- Transactions complete entirely or fail completely
4. **Micro-economics** -- Transaction costs must scale to $0.001 amounts

## Agents as Economic Actors

Agents function as economic entities with wallets, reputations, and financial relationships. 3,760 successful cross-chain transactions without intervention demonstrates viability for machine-to-machine commerce at scale.
