# AI Diagnoses Better Than Doctors
**Author:** Tim Green  **Published:** February 9, 2026

## Overview
Comprehensive analysis examining the paradox of AI diagnostic capabilities outperforming human physicians while patient trust remains conditional. Synthesizes research showing AI systems achieving 85-92% diagnostic accuracy in specific domains, yet explores why public acceptance lags behind technical performance.

## Key Concepts
- Diagnostic Error Burden: approximately 795,000 Americans annually experience harm from diagnostic mistakes
- AI Performance: ChatGPT Plus achieves 92% accuracy on complex cases versus 73.7% for conventional physician approaches
- Trust Paradox: despite superior performance data, patients consistently prefer human doctors, followed by human-AI collaboration, with AI-alone coming last
- Regulatory Framework: FDA approved 235 AI devices in 2024; 97% cleared via 510(k) pathway without independent clinical validation
- Equity Concerns: AI training data perpetuates historical healthcare disparities, particularly affecting Black and Hispanic populations
- Integration Reality: major healthcare systems deploying AI with claims of 42% diagnostic error reduction
