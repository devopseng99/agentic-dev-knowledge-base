# Understanding the SEC's Guidelines on AI Governance: What You Need to Know
**Author:** Essertinc  **Published:** July 1, 2025

## Overview
The SEC expects companies to treat AI-related risks with the same rigor as cybersecurity, financial controls, and ESG. AI influences algorithmic trading, portfolio modeling, automated underwriting, and anomaly detection — each presenting substantial consequences when systems malfunction.

## Key Concepts

**SEC Guidelines — Five Core Areas**
1. **10-K/10-Q Disclosures** — Mandatory reporting of AI systems affecting operations, decision-making, or risk management
2. **Board Oversight** — Establishing AI governance subcommittees with quarterly briefings
3. **Material Risk Reporting** — Disclosing AI incidents under Reg S-K
4. **Cybersecurity Integration** — AI-powered threat detection must comply with cybersecurity disclosure requirements
5. **ESG Alignment** — Demonstrating ethical technology use and stakeholder fairness

**Key Challenges**
- Data bias and explainability in complex models
- Model Risk Management (MRM) inadequate for adaptive AI systems
- Fragmented governance across IT, legal, and compliance silos
- Documentation gaps lacking audit trails and version controls

**Six-Step Implementation Framework**
1. Create comprehensive AI system inventory with risk classification
2. Adopt governance policies aligned with FATE principles (Fairness, Accountability, Transparency, Explainability)
3. Document complete model development lifecycles
4. Conduct independent bias and robustness testing
5. Deploy board-level governance dashboards
6. Plan regulatory disclosure responses

**Future Outlook:** Anticipated dedicated AI governance disclosure rules, explainability requirements, and global alignment through NIST AI RMF.
