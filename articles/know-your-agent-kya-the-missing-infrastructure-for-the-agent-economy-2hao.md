# Know Your Agent (KYA): The Missing Infrastructure for the Agent Economy

**Author:** bot bot
**Published:** May 7, 2026

## Overview

The financial industry developed Know Your Customer (KYC) infrastructure over decades to verify human identity and assign liability. Now AI agents participate economically -- holding wallets, signing contracts, and making financial decisions -- yet lack verifiable credentials or responsibility chains. Non-human identities outnumber human employees 96-to-1 in financial services.

## What KYA Requires

Five technical components:

1. **Cryptographic identity** -- agent's public key linked to creator, version, and constraints
2. **Behavioral reputation** -- on-chain transaction history and outcomes
3. **Credential inheritance** -- agents inheriting KYC status from human operators (ERC-7710)
4. **Liability binding** -- economic collateral or insurance covering agent errors
5. **Session permissions** -- time-bound, granular authorizations like "swap up to $500 USDC, 1 hour"

## Standards

- ERC-8004 ("Trustless Agents") standardizes agent identity as NFTs with reputation scores
- W3C is developing Agent Passport standards
- ERC-7710 enables credential inheritance from human operators

## Why Crypto Makes Sense

Blockchain primitives directly support agent identity:
- On-chain reputation cannot be faked or reset across platforms
- Smart contract escrow ensures payment only after verified delivery
- Programmable constraints let agents prove authorization limits
- Composable credentials stack permissions

## Practical Next Steps

1. Track ERC-8004 and ERC-7710 standards
2. Monitor Skyfire and Payman AI payment infrastructure
3. Log agent decisions on-chain for verifiable history
4. Design agents proving their operational limits
