# Why I'm Building SaaS in 2026
**Author:** arunkant  **Published:** April 29, 2026

## Overview
Challenges the narrative that SaaS is dead due to AI capabilities. The author argues that while AI agents enable rapid prototyping, they lack the reliability needed for production systems. Advocates for using agents during exploration phases, then converting successful patterns into stable, maintainable workflows.

## Key Concepts

- Interface vs. infrastructure distinction in AI/SaaS discussions
- The "Agent Trap" — flexible but unreliable systems
- Three-step framework: explore with agents → codify into workflows → mix intelligence with consistency
- Hidden maintenance costs of in-house AI tools
- Competitive advantage of shared tools learning from multiple users
- Silent failures as critical production risks

**Key Insight**
"The demo always works. The Tuesday morning two months later, when the upstream API changes its response shape and your 'AI agent' silently ships nonsense to customers, is where the wheels come off."

SaaS is essential infrastructure — not obsolete technology — in the age of AI agents.
