# AI in Brazilian Healthcare: How Small Tech Teams Are Solving Problems Big Pharma Can't
**Author:** José Gonçalves  **Published:** February 13, 2026

## Overview
Explores how lean technology teams in Brazil are driving healthcare innovation rather than multinational pharmaceutical corporations. Examines Brazil's SUS (Sistema Único de Saúde) public healthcare system serving 190+ million people and highlights specific technological challenges creating opportunities for local developers.

## Key Concepts
- Telemedicine Growth: consultations expanded over 300% during/after COVID-19, built primarily by small tech companies
- Brazilian Healthcare Requirements: LGPD compliance, SUS database integration, TISS/TUSS standards, Portuguese-language NLP, low-bandwidth optimization, multi-device support
- AI Applications: clinical decision support for primary care, medical image analysis with radiologist queue prioritization, patient flow optimization in emergency departments, real-time drug interaction checking
- Critical Challenges: data quality, evolving ANVISA AI guidelines, varying digital literacy, unreliable connectivity requiring offline capabilities
- Recommended Stack: Python (FastAPI), Node.js, PyTorch, Hugging Face, ONNX, PostgreSQL with encryption, Redis, AWS São Paulo region or Azure, HL7 FHIR, TISS/TUSS integration standards
