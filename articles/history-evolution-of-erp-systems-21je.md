# History & Evolution of ERP Systems
**Author:** Bhavesh Gangani  **Published:** April 2, 2025

## Overview
Traces the evolution of ERP systems from 1960s Material Requirements Planning through modern AI-integrated platforms.

## Key Concepts

**1960s: MRP Origins**
Businesses started using Material Requirements Planning to manage manufacturing processes — tracking inventory, production schedules, and raw material procurement through automated computational processes.

**1970s: MRP II Evolution**
Manufacturing Resource Planning expanded to encompass scheduling, capacity planning, and shop floor control, integrating financial data with production planning.

**1980s: The ERP Transition**
Systems broadened beyond manufacturing to incorporate financial, HR, and other business functions. Software vendors began specializing in enterprise solutions across multiple industries.

**1990s: Mainstream Adoption**
Major vendors including SAP, Oracle, and PeopleSoft developed comprehensive platforms:
- Modular systems with flexible deployment
- CRM and supply chain management integration
- Cross-industry expansion (retail, healthcare, finance)
- Customization capabilities with scalability

**2000s: Cloud & Accessibility**
Web-based solutions enabled remote access and real-time collaboration. Cloud computing reduced infrastructure costs; mobile ERP expanded operational flexibility.

**2010s-Present: Modern Technologies**
- AI/ML for predictive analytics
- IoT integration
- Mobile accessibility
- Industry-specific solutions

**Future Outlook**
- AI-driven automation
- Blockchain integration
- IoT connectivity
- AR/VR interfaces
- Enhanced cybersecurity measures
