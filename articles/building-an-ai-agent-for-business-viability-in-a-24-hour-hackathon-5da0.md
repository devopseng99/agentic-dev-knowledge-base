# Building an AI Agent for Business Viability In a 24 Hour Hackathon
**Author:** Favor Charles Owuor  **Published:** February 5, 2026

## Overview
A five-person team developed Microbiz, an AI-powered tool assessing whether business ideas are financially viable. The system combines deterministic financial calculations with AI reasoning to help entrepreneurs evaluate ideas before investing resources.

## Key Concepts

- **Problem Space:** Most entrepreneurs lack data-driven validation before committing capital to business ventures
- **Core Architecture:** Deterministic calculation engine (revenue, costs, margins) paired with AI reasoning layer
- **AI Integration:** Groq AI API used for higher-level feasibility assessment and market condition analysis
- **GIGO Principle:** "Garbage In Garbage Out" — incorrect assumptions produce confidently wrong AI outputs
- **Team Structure:** Clear role separation (backend, frontend, prompt engineering) reduced chaos during development
- **Key Insight:** "AI agents amplify systems. They do not replace solid foundations"

**Technical Stack**
- Backend: Django
- AI Provider: Groq AI
- Focus: Input validation, financial calculations, data structuring for AI consumption

**Hackathon Lesson**
"AI could not be the product by itself. It had to support a real system." The AI layer was valuable precisely because it sat on top of deterministic calculations — it added interpretation without replacing accuracy.
