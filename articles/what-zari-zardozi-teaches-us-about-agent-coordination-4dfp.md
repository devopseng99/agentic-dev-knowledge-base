# What Zari-Zardozi Teaches Us About Agent Coordination
**Author:** Talvinder Singh  **Published:** March 18, 2026

## Overview
Draws parallels between a 400-year-old Indian embroidery craft and modern multi-agent AI system architecture. Traditional command-and-control planning models for coordinating AI agents are inefficient. Proposes "Layered Autonomy" as an alternative approach inspired by how Zari-Zardozi artisans work together.

## Key Concepts
1. **Layered Autonomy** — Workers operate on shared context with loose temporal coupling but tight coherence, rather than sequential task chains
2. **Specialization Without Silos** — Agents maintain distinct roles while accessing shared understanding of the complete design
3. **Shared Context as Infrastructure** — Coordination happens through access to evolving artifacts rather than message-passing between agents
4. **Iterative Refinement Over Planning** — Phase-based execution allows adaptation rather than attempting perfect upfront decomposition
5. **Orchestrator as Quality Guardian** — Leadership validates transitions and maintains vision without micromanaging individual tasks

Performance claims: Layered autonomy systems demonstrate "at least 40% improvement in both speed and quality" compared to planner-orchestrated approaches.
