# After 100 hours: what open-source humanoid hardware lets tinkerers actually experiment with
**Author:** Pomeloid  **Published:** April 15, 2026

## Overview
Menlo Research developed Asimov, an open-source humanoid robot that democratizes robotics research. The "Here Be Dragons Edition" DIY kit costs $15,000 — close to manufacturing expenses — enabling independent builders to conduct locomotion experiments previously restricted to well-funded labs.

## Key Concepts
- **Open Architecture:** Mechanical designs, simulation models, and control algorithms are publicly available
- **Modular Design:** Legs, torso, arms, and head connect via universal motor fixtures for independent subsystem development
- **Cost Accessibility:** Leg subsystem ~$10,000; full robot estimated at ~$30,000
- **Sim-to-Real Transfer:** Includes parallel in-the-loop simulator with latency injection matching physical hardware behavior
- **Custom Integration:** Modular design facilitates sensor additions without warranty concerns

## Specifications
- Height: 1.20 m
- Weight: ~35 kg
- Degrees of Freedom: 25+2 (including articulated toes)
- Build Time: ~100 hours (R&D pace)
