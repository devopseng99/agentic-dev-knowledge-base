# Why I built a Physical Kill-Switch Protocol for AI Safety (Open Source)
**Author:** Ahmed Mohamed Abdel-Maaboud  **Published:** May 2, 2026

## Overview
The article presents AEGIS-HARDWIRE, a hardware-based safety protocol designed to ensure physical control over robotic systems regardless of software sophistication. The author argues that "true safety must be physical" rather than relying solely on software solutions.

## Key Concepts
- **Hardware-First Safety:** Moving safety mechanisms from software layers to circuit-level implementations
- **Physical Kill-Switch Protocol:** A mechanism enabling manual shutdown independent of system intelligence
- **Open Source Approach:** Code available for community audit, modification, and improvement
- **System Architecture Focus:** Addressing safety through infrastructure design rather than algorithmic constraints
