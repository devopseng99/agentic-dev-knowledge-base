# I Tried Selling AI to Law Firms. It Was a Disaster. Here's What Worked Instead.
**Author:** automatedprospect  **Published:** (unavailable — page returned 404)

## Overview
Article discusses the challenges of selling AI solutions directly to law firms — a conservative market. The author found that breaking into the legal tech space requires an indirect approach through trusted vendors who already have relationships with law firms.

## Key Concepts

**The Direct Sales Problem**
Law firms are conservative institutions with strong preferences for established vendors, established technologies, and established relationships. Cold outreach with AI solutions tends to fail because:
- Legal professionals are risk-averse by nature
- AI carries reputational risk for legal practice
- Trust is paramount in legal engagements

**What Worked Instead**
The successful approach: work through trusted intermediaries and existing legal software vendors who already have the "key to law firms." Positioning AI as an enhancement to existing trusted tools rather than a standalone disruptive product.

**Key Lessons for Legal Tech Sales**
- Conservative markets require indirect sales approaches
- Trust is built through partners, not cold pitches
- Frame AI as risk reduction, not disruption
- Legal professionals respond better to proven ROI than innovation claims
