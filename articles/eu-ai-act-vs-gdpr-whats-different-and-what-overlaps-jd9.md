# EU AI Act vs GDPR: What's Different and What Overlaps
**Author:** Guillermo Llopis  **Published:** March 5, 2026

## Overview
GDPR compliance alone is insufficient for EU AI Act compliance. Both regulations share risk-based thinking and documentation requirements but have fundamentally different purposes, scopes, and mechanisms. August 2026 deadline for AI Act compliance.

## Key Concepts

**Different Legal Purposes**
- GDPR: Protecting personal data rights
- EU AI Act: Product safety and system transparency (applies even without personal data processing)

**Areas of Genuine Overlap**
1. Transparency Requirements — GDPR requires data processing notices; AI Act demands system capability documentation
2. Risk Assessments — GDPR requires DPIA; AI Act requires FRIA (Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment). Do DPIA first, then build FRIA from that foundation.
3. Automated Decision-Making — GDPR Article 22 provides individual rights; AI Act mandates human oversight features for high-risk systems
4. Data Governance — AI Act adds training dataset requirements (representativeness, completeness) for high-risk systems

**AI Act-Specific Requirements (No GDPR Equivalent)**
| Requirement | Scope |
|---|---|
| Risk classification system | Four-tier categorization |
| Annex IV technical documentation | Far more detailed than GDPR records |
| Conformity assessment | Self-assessment or notified body |
| CE marking | Required for high-risk systems |
| Post-market monitoring | Ongoing product surveillance |
| EU database registration | Article 71 compliance tracking |

**Recommended Compliance Actions**
1. Map all AI systems against both frameworks
2. Integrate impact assessments rather than treating them separately
3. Extend documentation beyond GDPR Article 30 minimum
4. Classify systems under AI Act risk categories
5. Recognize AI Act obligations as distinct from data protection requirements
