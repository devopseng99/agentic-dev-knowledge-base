# AI Governance in Law Firms: Proactive Compliance Strategies
**Author:** Josh Lee  **Published:** October 10, 2025

## Overview
The EU AI Act and UK regulations make AI governance mandatory for law firms. Successful firms should establish governance frameworks proactively rather than waiting for external requirements.

## Key Concepts

**Establishing AI Governance**
Foundation requires: clear policies, approval steps, oversight teams, and alignment with risk management approach.

**Defining AI Usage Policies**
- Categorize AI tools by legal function (research, document review, client communication)
- Strict data confidentiality rules preventing confidential client information in public tools
- Prohibited uses: uploading documents to unsecured platforms, using AI for final legal opinions without human review, filing court documents without verification

**AI Approval Workflows**
- Risk-based approval tiers (low, medium, high risk)
- Cross-functional review teams (IT security, ethics counsel)
- 5-10 business day review periods with documented decision rationale

**Oversight Committee Composition**
- Managing partner (project approval authority)
- IT director (security and data protection)
- Ethics counsel (professional responsibility compliance)
- Practice group leaders (daily use monitoring)
- Quarterly meeting cadence with monthly reporting to senior leadership

**Required Documentation**
- AI usage policies with version dates
- Staff training certificates
- Tool approval and risk assessment forms
- Incident reports and responses
- Client consent forms

**Monitoring Schedule**
- Weekly: Review new AI tool requests
- Monthly: Staff compliance audits
- Quarterly: Incident response testing
- Annually: Full policy reviews
