# The Solo-Founder Playbook: Zero to Hero
**Author:** Truong Phung  **Published:** 2026-05-04

## Overview
Comprehensive guide for software entrepreneurs building alone, covering the journey from concept through $1M ARR. "Distribution kills you, not product."

## Key Concepts

### Core Principles
1. **Distribution over product:** Marketing/distribution is the #1 challenge; successful indie hackers cite distribution as the deciding factor.
2. **Validation precedes development:** Pre-sell or secure customer commitment before writing production code.
3. **Boring technology wins:** Stack choice matters less than iteration speed and distribution capability.
4. **Solo founders are leveraged humans:** Cut everything that doesn't generate revenue, retention, or distribution.
5. **Energy management over time management:** 54% burnout rate makes sustainability the real competitive advantage.

### Idea Selection: Five-Filter Test

| Filter | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| Pain Severity | Can you find 20 paying customers in one week? |
| Reachable Market | Is there a channel with 10K+ target customers? |
| Willingness to Pay | Will 3 prospects pre-commit money before launch? |
| Solo-Buildable | Can you build v1.0 alone in 12 weeks? |
| Five-Year Interest | Will this domain sustain your engagement? |

### Validation Hierarchy (weakest to strongest)
- Surveys and email signups (1 star)
- Fake-door button clicks (3 stars)
- Letters of intent (4 stars)
- Stripe deposits/pre-orders (5 stars)
- Recurring payments from strangers (6 stars)

### Six-Week MVP Budget

| Week | Deliverable |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Auth, data model, empty state |
| 2 | Single core workflow (50% polish) |
| 3 | Second workflow, payments, pricing |
| 4 | Polish, analytics, error handling |
| 5 | Beta to pre-order list, daily fixes |
| 6 | Public launch, first cohort onboarding |

### Boring Stack 2026
- Frontend: Next.js 15 + TypeScript + Tailwind
- Backend: Next.js API routes or Hono on Cloudflare Workers
- Database: PostgreSQL (Supabase or Neon)
- Auth: Supabase Auth or Clerk
- Payments: Stripe
- Hosting: Vercel or Render
- Cost: $0–$50/month until 100 users

### Distribution Channel Comparison

| Channel | Time-to-Customer | Compounding | Solo-Suitable |
|---------|------------------|------------|--------------|
| SEO/Long-form | 6–12 months | 5 stars | 5 stars |
| X/Twitter (build in public) | 2–8 weeks | Good | 5 stars |
| LinkedIn (B2B) | 4–12 weeks | Very good | 4 stars |
| Cold outbound | 1–4 weeks | Linear | 3 stars |
| Paid ads | 1–4 weeks | None | 2 stars |

### Pricing Framework
- Price based on value delivered minus alternative cost, not competitor pricing
- Three-tier monthly + annual (annual = 2 months free)
- At <$30K MRR, founders consistently undercharge
- Raising 30–50% typically loses <10% of customers while yielding 20–35% revenue lift

### Realistic First-Month Conversion
100 outreaches → 8–15 calls → 3–5 trials → 1–3 paying customers

### Growth Stages
- **$0 → $10K MRR (6–18 months):** Find product-channel fit — 1 channel, 1 ICP, 1 product
- **$10K → $100K MRR (12–24 months):** Repeat what works — 2x best channel before adding second
- **$100K → $1M ARR (18+ months):** Expand carefully — one major bet per quarter

### Key Takeaways
- Distribution determines success far more than product quality
- Validate before building; pre-sell before validating
- Solo founder economics: $1M ARR + 70% margin = $700K/yr take-home
- AI leverage is now table-stakes: 25–50 hours/week of compounding work possible
