# Data Privacy in AI Mental Health Tools: What You Should Know
**Author:** bvanderbilt0033  **Published:** July 3, 2025

## Overview
Examines data privacy concerns surrounding AI-powered mental health applications. While these tools increase accessibility to mental health support, they simultaneously create risks around sensitive personal information. Balances benefits of AI in mental health with critical privacy considerations.

## Key Concepts
- AI in Mental Health Growth: 36% of psychologists recommend AI tools for mild-to-moderate anxiety and depression (Woebot, Wysa, Replika, Abby.gg)
- Data Collection Types: text/voice inputs, biometric data, location information, emotion recognition via facial expressions or voice analysis
- Privacy Risks: data breaches, third-party sharing (29 of 36 apps studied shared data without proper consent), inadequate anonymization, opaque privacy policies
- Regulatory Landscape: HIPAA, GDPR, and CCPA provide some protections, but many consumer apps fall outside healthcare provider regulations
- Best Practices: encryption, transparency, data minimization, privacy-by-design approaches
