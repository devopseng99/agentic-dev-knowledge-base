# Robot Training Data Is Turning Labor Into Infrastructure
**Author:** Simon Paxton  **Published:** April 12, 2026

## Overview
The article examines how human labor is being converted into training data for humanoid robots. Workers wearing head-mounted cameras record tasks like towel-folding, generating first-person video datasets that become valuable AI infrastructure.

## Key Concepts
1. **Labor as Infrastructure** - Workers become upstream input producers in AI supply chains rather than final-product manufacturers
2. **First-Person Video Data** - Head-mounted cameras capture perception and decision-making context that third-person observation misses, making footage more valuable for embodied AI training
3. **Uneven Value Distribution** - Workers face immediate burdens (surveillance, repeated takes, potential automation) while upside accrues to data firms, robot companies, and integrators
4. **Data as Competitive Moat** - Proprietary task-specific training datasets may become harder to acquire than commodity hardware or generic models
5. **Extraction Over Replacement** - Present-day data collection matters more economically than future automation scenarios

Key quote: "Labor is shifting from producing tangible goods to producing machine-readable training assets."
