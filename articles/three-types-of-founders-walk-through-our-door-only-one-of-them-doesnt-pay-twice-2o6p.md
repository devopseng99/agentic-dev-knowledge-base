# Three types of founders walk through our door. Only one of them doesn't pay twice.
**Author:** Paweł Reszka  **Published:** 2026-05-07

## Overview
After auditing over 50 AI-built codebases, three founder archetypes are identified with their distinct financial outcomes when building software products.

## Key Concepts

### The First Founder: The Fast Builder
Rapidly builds MVP using Lovable or Replit. Product launches with polished UI and solid features. But when hiring developers, discovers critical flaws: "database security policies wide open, no payment verification on Stripe webhooks, every API endpoint accepting requests from any website on the internet, credentials hardcoded in the source, and zero tests."

**Cost Impact:** Rescue and migration typically exceeds $10,000-25,000 for an MVP that originally cost $200 in platform credits.

### The Second Founder: The Cost Minimizer
Hires teams at $15-20/hour rates. Initial demos impress, but production reveals systemic problems. Architecture doesn't scale; code is duplicated across files; no consistent error handling, logging, or deployment pipeline.

**Cost Impact:** Total spending reaches $35,000-65,000 (initial failed version plus complete rebuild).

### The Third Founder: The Methodical Builder
Invests upfront in proper development, prioritizing production-readiness from day one: security policies, payment verification, test coverage, clean architecture, no vendor lock-in.

**Cost Impact:** Single investment of $12,000-30,000 for a sustainable, maintainable product.

### Market Context (2026)
Two years prior: senior-team MVPs cost $40,000-60,000 over 4-6 months.
In 2026: AI-assisted development enables senior developers to deliver 2-3x faster. Production-ready MVPs now cost $12,000-25,000 in 6-8 weeks.

"The smart approach is no longer the expensive option. It's the only one that makes financial sense."

### Main Takeaway
The financial gap between rushed and methodical approaches has inverted — making proper development the most economically rational choice.
