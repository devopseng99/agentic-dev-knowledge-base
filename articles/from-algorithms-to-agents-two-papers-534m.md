# From Algorithms to Agents: Two Papers
**Author:** haseeb arif  **Published:** May 6, 2026

## Overview
Analysis of two 2025 research papers representing contrasting approaches to AI advancement: emergent agentic systems (a broad survey) and refined classical algorithms (adaptive A* for autonomous vehicles).

## Key Concepts

### Paper 1: Agentic AI Review

**Full Citation:** Bandi et al., "The Rise of Agentic AI: A Review of Definitions, Frameworks, Architectures, Applications, Evaluation Metrics, and Challenges," *Future Internet* 17(9):404 (2025)

**Five Pillars of Agentic AI:**
1. Planning — decomposing goals into sub-tasks
2. Memory — retaining context across interactions
3. Tool use — interacting with external systems
4. Reflection — self-evaluating and correcting
5. Collaboration — coordinating with other agents

**Key Statistics:**
- Over 90% of surveyed papers published 2024-2025 (rapidly emerging field)
- Applications span education, healthcare, transportation, software engineering

**Critical Gap Identified:**
No standardized evaluation benchmarks exist for agentic systems — a significant barrier to comparative research and responsible deployment.

### Paper 2: Adaptive A* Algorithm

**Full Citation:** "Research on the A* Algorithm Based on Adaptive Weights and Heuristic Reward Values," *World Electric Vehicle Journal* 16(3):144 (2025)

**Key Innovations:**
- Diagonal-free five-way directional search
- Context-sensitive adaptive weighting function
- Heuristic reward values preventing corner-collision hazards
- Reduces node expansion while maintaining path quality

### Unifying Principle
Both papers demonstrate a common pattern: contextually intelligent systems that adapt behavior based on environmental specifics rather than applying fixed strategies. The agentic AI framework's "reflection" pillar parallels the adaptive algorithm's dynamic weight adjustment.

### Evaluation Metrics Gap
The survey's finding that no standardized agent evaluation benchmarks exist is the most actionable research gap — and is already being addressed by projects like GAIA, AgentBench, and WebArena, though consensus remains elusive.
