# How AI Is Disrupting The Legal Field Changing Past Practices and Shaping the Future
**Author:** Josh Lee  **Published:** October 2, 2025

## Overview
Legal work traditionally involved "hours digging through documents, researching cases, and drafting contracts." AI now automates routine tasks, allowing lawyers to focus on strategy rather than paperwork.

## Key Concepts

**Before and After AI in Legal Work**

Legal Research:
- Before: Manual research through law libraries, printed journals, physical case files
- Now: AI-driven tools scan databases using NLP in seconds

Contract Review:
- Before: Line-by-line manual review taking days or weeks
- Now: AI flags risks, suggests improvements, auto-fills clauses instantly

Case Prediction:
- Before: Gut-feeling predictions
- Now: AI models crunch huge datasets of past outcomes for probability scoring

Legal Discovery:
- Before: Manual sorting through emails and documents for weeks
- Now: AI-powered e-discovery analyzes data including audio and video files rapidly

**Emerging AI-Driven Innovations**

Automated Compliance and Risk Assessment:
- AI scans thousands of regulations and identifies compliance issues automatically
- Flags problematic clauses and regulatory conflicts

AI-Powered Chatbots and Virtual Assistants:
- Modern versions understand context and guide clients through forms
- Virtual assistants manage schedules, draft routine documents, summarize case information

AI's Role in Enhancing Access to Justice:
- Online tools provide free or low-cost legal guidance
- AI simplifies complex paperwork for underserved populations
- Democratizes legal assistance for those previously unable to afford services

**Key Takeaways**
1. Automation of repetitive legal tasks
2. Enhanced accuracy and risk detection
3. Improved access to justice through technology
4. Time and cost reduction
5. Shift in lawyer focus from grunt work to strategic analysis
