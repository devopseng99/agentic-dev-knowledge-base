# Why Humanoid Robots Still Struggle in 2026: Data, Dexterity, and Generality
**Author:** Humanoid Guide  **Published:** March 28, 2026

## Overview
The article examines three fundamental bottlenecks limiting humanoid robot progress despite impressive recent demonstrations. The author, conducting robotics research since 2014, argues that attention-grabbing demos mask deeper challenges in real-world deployment.

## Key Concepts

1. **Data Scarcity** — Real-world robot training demands expensive physical demonstrations, teleoperation logs, and failure cases. Simulation-to-reality transfer remains problematic across varying environments.

2. **Dexterous Manipulation** — Hand capability represents the critical limitation. Tasks like cable-handling, packaging manipulation, and tool-use require integrated hardware-software solutions combining degrees of freedom, force control, and tactile sensing.

3. **Generalization vs. Demos** — Systems excelling in curated tasks often fail at transferring skills, handling novel objects, or operating reliably at commercial scale in unstructured environments.

4. **Systems Integration** — Success requires cross-stack expertise spanning mechanics, controls, perception, data infrastructure, and learning architectures.
