# Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback
**Author:** Paperium  **Published:** December 23, 2025

## Overview
Constitutional AI is a methodology for developing AI systems that maintain ethical behavior through feedback mechanisms rather than exclusively human supervision. AI systems can evaluate their own outputs against constitutional guidelines, leveraging AI feedback to identify and eliminate harmful responses at scale.

## Key Concepts

### Core Process
The method involves training models using a two-phase approach:
1. **Phase 1 (SL-CAI):** AI systems generate responses to various prompts, then evaluate those responses against predefined constitutional principles. The self-critique generates training signals that improve model behavior.
2. **Phase 2 (RL-CAI):** Reinforcement learning from AI feedback using preference data generated by comparing responses against the constitution.

### Constitutional Principles
The system operates according to established rules or guidelines. These principles guide both the evaluation process and model refinement, creating consistency in how harmfulness gets assessed. The constitution covers dimensions such as:
- Non-deception
- Non-manipulation
- Harmlessness
- Helpfulness to society

### Significance
This methodology addresses scalability challenges in AI alignment. Traditional approaches requiring extensive human annotation become impractical as systems grow more sophisticated. Constitutional AI offers a pathway toward maintainable safety standards without proportional increases in human oversight requirements.

### Key Advantage
AI systems participate in their own improvement processes while adhering to explicit constitutional guidelines — enabling alignment at scale that pure human feedback cannot achieve economically.
