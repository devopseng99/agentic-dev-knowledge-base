# Why a half-marathon is a better robot benchmark than a warehouse demo
**Author:** Pomeloid  **Published:** April 14, 2026

## Overview
The article argues that a 21.1 km public half-marathon course provides superior robotics testing compared to controlled warehouse demonstrations. The inaugural 2025 Beijing humanoid robot half-marathon revealed critical failure modes that laboratory environments typically mask. Only 6 of 21 robots completed the race.

## Key Concepts
- **The Demo Problem:** Controlled environments hide engineering challenges and require extensive pre-testing
- **Real-World Stressors:** Multi-hour operation, varying terrain, sustained thermal loads, and mechanical durability
- **Standardized Benchmarking:** Public, multi-vendor testing creates transparent performance metrics
- **Autonomous Navigation:** New category measuring path-planning and obstacle response over extended distances
- **Completion Rate:** 2025 achieved 29% (6/21); 2026 has 300+ robots from 100+ teams, ~38% capable of autonomous navigation
