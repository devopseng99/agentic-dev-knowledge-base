# AI Agents That Matter
**Author:** Mike Young (aimodels-fyi)  **Published:** July 12, 2024 (edited January 18, 2026)

## Overview
This paper examines how AI agents should be evaluated responsibly and at scale. The research emphasizes developing "cost-controlled and scalable evaluations" rather than expensive, resource-intensive assessment methods. The focus is on creating agents that deliver meaningful real-world impact rather than excelling at narrow benchmarks.

## Key Concepts
- Cost-controlled evaluation frameworks for AI agents
- Scalable assessment methodologies
- Moving beyond isolated task performance to real-world impact
- Agent adaptation to novel situations
- Human-AI collaboration capabilities
- Alignment with human values and societal priorities

The article acknowledges the paper lacks practical implementation details and doesn't fully address potential trade-offs between cost efficiency and comprehensive evaluation rigor. Calls for "further research and experimentation" to refine the proposed framework.
